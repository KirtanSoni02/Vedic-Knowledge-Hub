Samhitas

Explore the ancient collections of mantras and verses that form the foundation of Vedic knowledge and spiritual wisdom.

Understanding Samhitas

Samhitas are the most ancient layer of Vedic texts, consisting of collections of mantras, hymns, prayers, and formulas. They represent the earliest documented layer of Vedic literature and form the foundation of Hindu philosophy and practice.

Origin & Etymology

The word "Samhita" comes from Sanskrit, meaning "put together, joined, composed in an orderly manner." These texts were meticulously compiled and preserved through oral tradition for thousands of years before being written down.

Cultural Significance

Samhitas hold immense cultural and religious significance as they contain the mantras used in Vedic rituals, philosophical concepts, and insights into ancient Indian society, cosmology, and spiritual practices.

History of Samhitas

1500 - 1200 BCE

Composition of the Rigveda Samhita, the oldest of the Vedic texts, containing hymns dedicated to deities and natural phenomena.

1200 - 900 BCE

Compilation of the Yajurveda Samhita, focusing on liturgical formulas and sacrificial rituals, divided into Krishna (Black) and Shukla (White) recensions.

1000 - 800 BCE

Development of the Samaveda Samhita, containing melodies and chants derived primarily from the Rigveda, used in Soma rituals and ceremonies.

900 - 700 BCE

Compilation of the Atharvaveda Samhita, containing spells, charms, and incantations for healing, prosperity, and addressing various life challenges.

Types of Samhitas

The oldest and most revered collection containing 1,028 hymns dedicated to various deities, organized into ten mandalas (books).
